Frequently Asked Questions

Are walk-in showers a good idea?

A walk-in shower offers a blend of immediate satisfaction and long-term utility, providing homeowners with increased floor space, enhanced lighting, easier maintenance, and a contemporary aesthetic.

Do you do the floor first or last in a bathroom remodel?

Deciding whether to tackle the bathroom floors first, which allows easier access to plumbing and subflooring, or to do them last for a cleaner installation of other bathroom elements, should consider factors such as budget, renovation project timeline, and personal preferences.

Do walk-in showers lower home value?

For enhancing resale value, the removal of a tub in favor of a well-appointed walk-in shower is a valuable home improvement, but when deciding between a bathtub and a walk-in shower, it's crucial to take into account the presence of another bathtub in the home.

Is Bathfitters worth the cost?

For many, the cost of a Bath Fitter® tub liner is justified as it offers enhanced durability and an attractive appearance to the bathroom, with the added benefit of a speedy installation in as little as two days, providing quicker access to the bathtub compared to traditional replacements.

How much does it cost to tile a shower?

The cost of tiling a shower, encompassing tiles, installation, and related materials, generally amounts to approximately $25 per square foot, resulting in an average total cost ranging from $450 to $1,000, with the final expense contingent on the tile selection and the shower's dimensions.
